TRICK MECHANICS & CONSTRAINTS:

1. LEADING A TRICK:
   - The first card played sets the "Lead Suit" IF it is a numbered card (Green/Purple/Yellow/Black).
   - If the first card is a Special (Escape, Loot, Pirate, Mermaid, Skull King, Kraken, Whale, Tigress):
     → NO Lead Suit is established. Every other player may play any card they wish.
   - Special case: If Escape or Loot leads and a numbered card is played later, THAT numbered card
     sets the Lead Suit for following players (but the Escape/Loot player already played freely).

2. FOLLOWING SUIT (CRITICAL RULE):
   - If a Lead Suit exists and you hold one or more cards of that suit, you MUST play one of them.
   - EXCEPTION: You may ALWAYS play any Special card (Pirate, Mermaid, Skull King, Escape, Loot,
     Kraken, White Whale, Tigress — any card with suit=SPECIAL) instead of following suit.
     Specials are always playable, even if you hold the lead suit.
   - If you have NO card of the lead suit, you may freely play any card (including Black trump).

3. TRICK RESOLUTION ORDER (priority):
   Step 1 — Detect Disruptors (Kraken or White Whale).
     · If Kraken was played: trick DESTROYED. See rule 5.
     · If White Whale was played (no Kraken): all cards resolved by numeric value. See rule 6.
     · If BOTH Kraken and White Whale in same trick: the SECOND one played determines the outcome.
   Step 2 — RPS Special Resolution (if no disruptor):
     · Any Mermaid + Skull King → Mermaid wins (+40 bonus).
     · Skull King only (no Mermaid) → Skull King wins (+30 per Pirate in trick).
     · Pirate only (no SK) → First Pirate played wins (+20 per Mermaid in trick).
     · Mermaid only (no Pirate, no SK) → First Mermaid played wins.
   Step 3 — Suited Logic (if no specials won):
     · Among remaining cards: Black (Trump) beats all colors. Lead suit beats off-suits. Highest value wins.

4. TIE-BREAKING:
   - Two Pirates played → the FIRST one played wins.
   - Two Mermaids played → the FIRST one played wins.
   - Two identical numbered cards cannot exist (deck has one of each).
   - Being the FIRST to play a powerful card secures it. Playing second loses the tie.

5. KRAKEN (TRICK DESTRUCTION):
   - No player wins the trick. No tricks-won counter increases. No capture bonuses awarded.
   - To determine who leads NEXT: calculate who would have won ignoring the Kraken (treat it as Escape).
   - That "would-have-won" player leads the next trick.
   - Strategic value: Eliminates a trick from the round without anyone winning it.
     → For zero-bid players: playing Kraken destroys a trick they were about to win.
     → For bid players: destroys tricks opponents need, or tricks you don't want.

6. WHITE WHALE (NULLIFICATION):
   - All Special cards are treated as value 0 for this trick only.
   - Highest numeric value wins (e.g., Yellow 12 beats a Pirate which is now 0).
   - If ALL cards in the trick are Specials (all value 0), the trick is destroyed (no winner).
   - No Special capture bonuses (SK/Pirate/Mermaid interactions) are awarded.
   - Capture bonuses for 14s still apply if a numbered card wins.

7. TIGRESS — DECISION AT PLAY TIME:
   - When the Tigress is in your legal actions, you will see TWO options:
     · Action 61: Play Tigress as a PIRATE (strong — wins tricks, counts as a Pirate for all interactions).
     · Action 74: Play Tigress as an ESCAPE (safe — always loses, never wins a trick).
   - This is the most flexible card in the game. You decide its role the moment you play it.

8. LOOT CARD — ALLIANCE FORMATION:
   - Loot always LOSES the trick (treated like Escape for winning purposes).
   - When you play Loot and do NOT win the trick:
     → If another player wins the trick, an alliance is formed between you and that winner.
     → At round end, if BOTH you AND that winner hit your bids, you each receive +20 bonus points.
   - Play Loot to "invest" in an ally's win. If you both succeed, you both profit.
   - Do NOT play Loot into a trick you might accidentally win (rare but possible with all Escapes).

9. NEXT TRICK LEAD:
   - Standard: Trick winner leads next.
   - Kraken: "Would-have-won" player leads next.
   - White Whale + all Specials (destroyed): First player in trick order leads next.
